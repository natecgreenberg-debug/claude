# Best Live Chat Software for Websites in 2026

Live chat has become table stakes for any business website. Studies consistently show that customers who chat before buying convert at higher rates and spend more per order. The problem: the market is flooded, pricing structures are confusing, and the "best" tool depends heavily on what you're trying to do.

I've spent time inside most of these platforms — either building them out for client sites or running them on my own. Here's a clear breakdown of what's actually worth your money in 2026.

---

## What to Look For in Live Chat Software

Before diving into specific tools, here's what matters:

**Response time and notifications.** If your agents miss chats because the notification system is clunky, the software is failing at its core job.

**Chatbot quality.** Most teams can't staff live chat 24/7. Good chatbots handle off-hours coverage, qualify leads, and route conversations.

**CRM integration.** Chat data is most valuable when it flows into your CRM automatically. Check what integrations exist before committing.

**Mobile apps.** Your team needs to handle chats from phones. Desktop-only is a dealbreaker for most businesses.

**Pricing transparency.** Some tools advertise low rates and then charge per agent per month in ways that balloon costs fast.

## The Best Live Chat Tools in 2026

### 1. Tidio — Best for Small Businesses and Ecommerce

Tidio has become one of the most popular small business live chat options for good reason: the free plan is genuinely useful, setup is fast, and the chatbot builder doesn't require a developer.

**What's good:**
- Free plan supports 50 live chat conversations per month and 100 chatbot conversations
- Drag-and-drop chatbot builder with pre-built templates for common use cases (abandoned cart recovery, lead capture, FAQ)
- Native integrations with Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress, and Wix
- Clean interface that agents actually enjoy using
- Mobile apps are solid on both iOS and Android

**What's not:**
- The free plan limits will bite you fast if you have real traffic
- Some of the more advanced AI features require the higher-tier plans
- Support response times can be slow on lower plans

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans start around $29/month. The "Tidio+" plan with full AI features runs $749/month — that's a significant jump, and not for small businesses.

**Best for:** Shopify/WooCommerce stores and small service businesses that want an affordable, reliable live chat without complexity.

---

### 2. LiveChat — Best for Teams That Need Professional Features

LiveChat is the veteran of this space. It's been around since 2002 and has built a genuinely mature product. If you're running a real customer support operation with multiple agents, this is the most polished option available.

**What's good:**
- Clean, fast agent interface — agents can handle multiple chats efficiently
- Strong reporting: chat satisfaction scores, first response times, queue wait times
- Excellent integration library (200+ integrations including Salesforce, HubSpot, Shopify, Slack)
- Good mobile apps
- Canned responses and tagging are well-implemented for managing high volume
- Chat routing and transfer between agents works smoothly

**What's not:**
- No free plan. Starts at $20/agent/month.
- The chatbot capabilities require a separate ChatBot product (additional cost)
- Can feel like overkill if you're a solo operator or tiny team

**Pricing:** Starter at $20/agent/month, Team at $41/agent/month, Business at $59/agent/month. All billed annually. Monthly rates are higher.

**Best for:** Growing teams with dedicated customer support needs. Businesses where chat volume is high enough to justify per-agent pricing.

---

### 3. Intercom — Best for Product-Led Growth Companies

Intercom is in a different category from the others — it's positioned as a "customer communications platform" more than just live chat. If you're a SaaS company that wants to tie chat into your product onboarding, user segmentation, and customer success, Intercom is the gold standard.

**What's good:**
- Powerful user segmentation: you can trigger messages based on what users have done in your app
- Excellent in-app chat and product tours
- Fin, their AI chatbot, is genuinely impressive at handling support queries
- The inbox management for high-volume support teams is mature
- Strong email + chat + product messaging integration

**What's not:**
- Expensive. Meaningfully expensive. Expect $74+/month to start, and pricing scales with seat count and usage in ways that add up fast
- Overkill for businesses without a software product
- The complexity means a real setup investment upfront

**Pricing:** Starts at $74/month for very small teams. Most growing companies pay $300-1,000+/month. They offer a 14-day free trial.

**Best for:** SaaS companies, subscription businesses, product-led growth. Not for ecommerce or simple service businesses.

---

### 4. Crisp — Best Value for Growing Teams

Crisp punches above its weight for the price. The free plan is one of the best available, and the paid plans are competitively priced for what you get.

**What's good:**
- Free plan: 2 agents, unlimited chat history, basic integrations
- Paid plans include a shared inbox for email, chat, and social messages — useful for small teams wearing multiple hats
- Built-in chatbot and knowledge base features on paid plans
- Nice UI that feels modern without being overcomplicated
- Good developer-friendly features if you want to customize behavior

**What's not:**
- The free plan is limited to 2 seats, which works for solos and very small teams only
- Chatbot capabilities aren't as sophisticated as Tidio or Intercom
- Some integrations require third-party tools to connect

**Pricing:** Free (2 agents). Pro at $25/month (4 agents). Unlimited at $95/month. Very reasonable.

**Best for:** Early-stage startups and small teams that want a capable shared inbox without Intercom prices.

---

### 5. Freshchat — Best for Larger Support Teams

Freshchat is part of the Freshworks ecosystem (which also includes Freshdesk, Freshsales, and others). If your team is already in Freshworks, it's a natural fit. Even standalone, it's a solid enterprise-grade live chat.

**What's good:**
- AI-powered chatbots that handle complex routing logic
- Strong integration with Freshdesk for ticket-based support
- Good multi-language support for international teams
- Analytics are detailed and actionable
- Scales well to large agent teams

**What's not:**
- Interface is denser and less intuitive than LiveChat or Tidio
- The free plan is limited to 10 agents and lacks some key features
- Best features require the Growth or above plans

**Pricing:** Free plan (10 agents, limited features). Growth at $19/agent/month. Pro at $49/agent/month.

**Best for:** Mid-to-large support teams, especially those already using Freshworks products.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Ecommerce store:** Tidio. The Shopify integration, chatbot templates for cart abandonment, and reasonable pricing make it the clear winner.

**Small service business:** Crisp's free plan or Tidio's free plan. Both work well for handling inbound inquiries without a budget.

**Growing team (5-20 agents):** LiveChat. The interface, reporting, and integration library justify the per-agent cost once you're past the scrappy phase.

**SaaS product:** Intercom, no question. It's expensive, but the product messaging, user segmentation, and AI support capabilities aren't replicated elsewhere.

**Already on Freshworks:** Freshchat is the obvious choice for the ecosystem fit.
