# Cheapest Email Marketing Platform with Automation in 2026

Email marketing with automation isn't a nice-to-have anymore — it's how you turn a list of subscribers into a real business. But finding a platform that handles automation well without charging enterprise prices is harder than it should be.

This guide covers the best budget email marketing platforms that actually have real automation features — not just "send an email after someone subscribes" but full sequences, conditional logic, and behavioral triggers.

Here's what I found when I tested the options most people are asking about.

---

## What "Real Automation" Actually Means

Before I get into the platforms, let me define what I'm looking for. "Automation" is used loosely in this space. Some platforms call a single welcome email an automation. That's not what this article is about.

Real email automation includes:
- Multi-step sequences (7-email welcome series, nurture sequences, etc.)
- Conditional branching (if subscriber clicks X, send Y; if they don't, send Z)
- Behavioral triggers (tag applied, link clicked, product purchased)
- Tagging and segmentation that updates dynamically
- Time delays and send-on-day-of-week controls

If a platform doesn't have conditional logic and behavioral triggers, it's not really automation — it's scheduled email.

## 1. Systeme.io — Best Free Automation Platform

**Free plan:** Up to 2,000 contacts, unlimited emails, 3 automation workflows
**Paid:** $27/month (Startup — 5,000 contacts), $47/month (Webinar), $97/month (Unlimited)
**Transaction fees:** None
**Verdict:** Best value at every price point

[Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) is the answer to the question "what's the cheapest platform with real automation?" and it's not particularly close.

The free plan includes automation workflows — not just scheduled sequences, but rule-based automations that trigger based on tags, form submissions, and purchases. For 2,000 contacts, that's a genuine free plan, not a demo.

The automation builder works with if/then logic: if a contact has a specific tag, enroll them in sequence A; if they click a link, add tag B and remove tag C; if they complete a sequence, move them to the next one. It's not as visually impressive as ConvertKit's automation builder, but the logic is there and it works.

What makes Systeme.io particularly interesting is the bundle. For $27/month, you're also getting funnel building, course hosting, and affiliate management. If you'd otherwise pay for those separately, the effective cost of the email automation component is near zero.

**Downsides:** The interface isn't as polished as dedicated email platforms. The visual automation builder is more limited than GetResponse or ConvertKit. If you want sophisticated visual funnels with complex branching, you'll feel the constraints.

**Best for:** Bootstrapped creators and solopreneurs who want automation plus a full suite of tools at minimal cost.

## 2. GetResponse — Best All-Round Budget Option for Automation Power

**Free plan:** Up to 500 contacts, basic email (no automation)
**Paid:** $19/month (Email Marketing — 1,000 contacts), $59/month (Marketing Automation), $119/month (E-commerce Marketing)
**Verdict:** Strong automation, good price — but automation requires the $59/month tier

GetResponse has been around for decades, and their automation tools are genuinely good. The marketing automation builder is visual, supports conditional logic, and includes triggers like "email opened," "link clicked," "tag added," and "purchase made."

The catch is that real automation (beyond basic autoresponders) is locked to the $59/month Marketing Automation plan. The $19/month Email Marketing plan does include basic sequences (autoresponders) but not the conditional workflow builder.

At $59/month for 1,000 contacts with full automation, GetResponse is competitive. At larger list sizes, the pricing scales reasonably.

**What GetResponse does particularly well:**
- Visual workflow builder with clear if/then branching
- Landing page builder included at all paid tiers
- Webinar hosting (unique among this category)
- E-commerce integrations for product recommendations

**Downsides:** The $19 entry plan is misleading for automation purposes — you need the $59 plan to get real conditional automations. The interface has also been updated so many times that it feels slightly inconsistent in places.

**Best for:** Businesses that want solid automation plus landing pages and potentially webinars, and have $60+/month to spend.

## 3. AWeber — Solid Basics, Now with AI Features

**Free plan:** Up to 500 subscribers, 3,000 emails/month (includes automations)
**Paid:** $15/month (Lite — 500 subscribers), $30/month (Plus — 2,500 subscribers), $65/month (Unlimited)
**Verdict:** Underrated free plan with basic automations; not the best for complex logic

AWeber has been around long enough that it sometimes gets dismissed as legacy software, but their free plan is genuinely useful and their automation features are solid for basic use cases.

The free plan includes:
- Email sequences (autoresponders)
- Landing pages
- Basic automation rules (subscribe to list → trigger sequence)
- AI email writing assistant (added recently)
- Email templates

What AWeber's free plan doesn't do well: complex conditional logic. You can trigger sequences based on subscription events, but branching automations based on link clicks or tag assignments require more manual setup compared to Systeme.io or GetResponse.

The paid plans are reasonably priced. At $30/month for up to 2,500 subscribers with full automation access, AWeber is competitive with the other mid-range options.

AWeber's deliverability reputation has historically been strong, and their support team is responsive — worth noting for platforms in this price range.

**Downsides:** The interface feels dated compared to newer platforms. The automation builder is functional but not visually elegant. If you're used to modern UX, AWeber will feel clunky.

**Best for:** Solopreneurs and small businesses who want a reliable, well-supported platform and primarily need sequence-style automations rather than complex branching.

## 4. ConvertKit — Best for Creators, Mid-Range Price

**Free plan:** Up to 10,000 subscribers, unlimited sends, no automation
**Paid:** $29/month (Creator — 300 subscribers), $59/month (Creator Pro)
**Verdict:** Excellent automation builder, but free plan has no automation

ConvertKit's visual automation builder is one of the best in this category. It's genuinely intuitive — you can see your entire funnel on one canvas, with sequences, conditional branches, and trigger events all connected visually.

The challenge for budget-focused buyers: automation is a paid feature. The free plan (which has a surprisingly high subscriber limit of 10,000) does not include automations. You need the paid Creator plan at $29/month to access sequences and the automation builder.

And here's the pricing quirk: the $29/month Creator plan only covers up to 300 subscribers. That feels backwards — you can have 10,000 subscribers for free but can't automate for them. The list size limits on paid plans are based on subscriber count, so as your list grows, your cost grows too.

At 1,000 subscribers, ConvertKit is around $49/month. At 5,000 subscribers, it's $79/month. That's more expensive than GetResponse or Systeme.io at similar list sizes.

**What ConvertKit does best:**
- Visual automation builder (one of the cleanest in the industry)
- Tag-based subscriber management (powerful segmentation)
- Creator-ecosystem integrations (Teachable, Thinkific, Podia)
- Commerce features (sell digital products directly)

**Best for:** Creators who are willing to pay a bit more for a better automation interface and creator-ecosystem integrations. Not the cheapest option, but the experience is polished.

## 5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Email Volume Pricing Model

**Free plan:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic automations
**Paid:** $25/month (Starter), $65/month (Business — full automations)
**Verdict:** Unique pricing model (pay per email, not per contact) — can be very cheap at low send volumes

Brevo prices by email volume rather than contact count. That's unusual and can work in your favor if you have a large list but send infrequently.

The free plan sends up to 300 emails/day (9,000/month) with unlimited contact storage and basic automations. That's a real free plan for a small but engaged list.

Advanced automations (conditional logic, behavioral triggers) require the $65/month Business plan. The Starter plan at $25/month has basic automations only.

**Best for:** Businesses with large contact lists that don't email frequently, or B2B senders with small active lists.
