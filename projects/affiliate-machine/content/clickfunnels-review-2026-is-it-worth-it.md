# ClickFunnels Review 2026: Is It Still Worth It? (Honest Take)

ClickFunnels was once the undisputed king of funnel builders. In 2026, the landscape looks very different — and whether ClickFunnels still deserves a spot in your stack depends heavily on what you're building and what you're willing to pay.

I've used ClickFunnels 2.0 across several client projects over the past year. Here's the real picture.

## Who ClickFunnels Is (and Isn't) For

Before we get into features and pricing, let's be honest about the target customer.

ClickFunnels is designed for **info product creators, coaches, and course sellers** who want an opinionated, all-in-one platform. The value proposition has always been: don't think about tech, just follow our system, sell your thing.

If you're building complex e-commerce, a SaaS product, or a multi-channel content business, ClickFunnels is probably not your tool. If you want to launch a course, a coaching offer, or a lead generation funnel fast, it has genuine merit.

## ClickFunnels 2.0: What Changed

ClickFunnels 2.0 (launched in 2022, still being iterated in 2024-2025) was a ground-up rebuild. The headline additions:

- Native course platform (no more Kajabi needed)
- Blog functionality (rudimentary, but it exists)
- CRM and pipeline management
- Email marketing built in
- Team collaboration features
- Better page speed (CF Classic was notoriously slow)

The rebuild was ambitious. The execution has been... uneven.

## What ClickFunnels Does Well

### Funnel Builder

The core funnel builder is still genuinely good. Drag-and-drop, hundreds of pre-built templates organized by funnel type (lead magnet, webinar, product launch, etc.). You can have a functional funnel live within a few hours.

The opinionated structure — squeeze page → thank you page → OTO → OTO downsell — is actually helpful if you're new to funnel strategy. The templates encode best practices.

### Community and Training

The ClickFunnels community is enormous. There are more tutorials, swipe files, and case studies for ClickFunnels funnels than any competing platform. If you get stuck, someone has solved your problem already.

Russell Brunson's training library (FunnelFlix) is included with most plans and is legitimately valuable for marketers. The strategy content alone has been worth the subscription for some users.

### Split Testing

A/B testing is solid and built in. Testing page variants, headlines, prices — it works reliably and the reporting is clear enough for non-technical marketers.

## What ClickFunnels Gets Wrong

### Pricing Is Hard to Justify

Here's where I have to be blunt: ClickFunnels is expensive relative to what you get.

- **Basic:** $147/month — 20 funnels, 1 website, 1 domain
- **Pro:** $197/month — 100 funnels, 3 domains, affiliate program access
- **Funnel Hacker:** $297/month — unlimited funnels, 9 domains, priority support

$147/month is the entry price for a tool with meaningful limitations. A new creator bootstrapping their first offer is going to feel this.

### ClickFunnels 2.0 Is Still Buggy

CF 2.0 launched before it was fully ready, and two years later, you still encounter issues. Pages that look different in the editor vs. published. Email sequences that don't trigger consistently. The course player has had stability complaints. The support response time has frustrated a lot of users.

This isn't theoretical — I've personally experienced broken checkout flows and had to roll back to a previous page version because an update broke the layout. For a premium-priced tool, this is unacceptable.

### The Feature Sprawl Problem

By trying to be everything — funnel builder, email, CRM, courses, blog, affiliate management — ClickFunnels has spread itself thin. None of the auxiliary features are best-in-class. The email marketing is functional but basic. The CRM is minimal. The blog SEO capabilities are thin.

If you're already paying for Klaviyo, a real CRM, and Kajabi or Teachable, you're duplicating costs without gaining much.

## Who Should Still Use ClickFunnels

- **Established info product businesses** already generating consistent revenue — the price is more manageable as a percentage of revenue
- **ClickFunnels ecosystem participants** — if you're selling courses about ClickFunnels, using it as a network play makes sense
- **Teams that need FunnelFlix training** — the strategy content has real value for marketing teams
- **Businesses where CF's template/community ecosystem provides ROI** — there are some verticals (real estate, local business) where CF-specific swipe files are extensive

## Who Should Look at Alternatives

- Early-stage creators watching every dollar
- Solopreneurs who don't need the overhead of CF's feature sprawl
- Anyone already paying for best-in-class email and CRM tools
- Creators whose main product is a membership or course (Kajabi/Teachable might be better fits)

## Final Verdict

ClickFunnels 2.0 is **not the clear choice it once was**. The price increase from the CF Classic days, combined with legitimate bugs and half-baked auxiliary features, has eroded the "just works" value proposition.

If you have the budget and you're deep in the ClickFunnels ecosystem, it still does the job. If you're evaluating fresh in 2026, the calculus has changed.
