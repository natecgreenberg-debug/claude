# Best Online Course Platform in 2026: Kajabi vs Teachable vs Systeme.io

The online education market keeps growing, and the platforms for hosting courses have matured significantly over the last few years. If you're building a course business in 2026, you have better options than ever — and the right choice depends heavily on where you are in your journey and what you actually need.

I've looked closely at the three platforms I get asked about most: Kajabi, Teachable, and Systeme.io. Here's what I found.

---

## What to Look For in a Course Platform

Before comparing specific tools, it's worth defining what actually matters:

- **Course hosting** — video storage, lessons, modules, quizzes
- **Student experience** — how easy is it for students to access and progress through your content
- **Payment processing** — one-time, subscription, and payment plan options
- **Marketing tools** — landing pages, email, funnels, upsells
- **Affiliate management** — can students and partners promote your courses
- **Pricing** — platform fees, transaction fees, monthly costs
- **Scalability** — can it grow with you from 10 students to 10,000

---

## Kajabi — The Premium All-in-One

**Pricing:** $89/month (Basic), $199/month (Growth), $399/month (Pro)
**Transaction fees:** None
**Best for:** Established creators who want everything in one place

Kajabi is the platform you graduate to when you're serious about your course business and have revenue to support the investment. It's genuinely excellent — polished, feature-rich, and designed around the creator's full business rather than just the course hosting piece.

**What Kajabi does well:**

The course builder is one of the best available. You can create structured learning paths with modules, lessons, and quizzes. The student portal is clean and professional. Video is hosted through their infrastructure, so you're not dealing with Vimeo or Wistia bills separately.

Beyond the course itself, Kajabi includes:
- Email marketing with visual automation builder
- Website and landing page builder
- Podcast hosting
- Community features
- Affiliate management
- Analytics across all your products

The value proposition is consolidation: instead of paying for ConvertKit + Thinkific + ClickFunnels + Zapier to wire them together, you have everything in one system. That has real value — less technical friction, better native integrations, and one support team.

**Where Kajabi falls short:**

The price. At $89/month minimum, Kajabi is not a starter platform. And the Basic plan has real limitations — only 3 products and 3 funnels. For most creators, the Growth plan at $199/month is the practical starting point.

The interface can also feel overwhelming at first. There's a lot of functionality packed in, and the learning curve is steeper than Teachable or Systeme.io.

**Who Kajabi is right for:**
Creators already making $2,000+/month who want to consolidate their tech stack and reduce integration headaches.

---

## Teachable — The Student-Focused Middle Ground

**Pricing:** Free (limited), $39/month (Basic), $119/month (Pro), $199/month (Pro+)
**Transaction fees:** 10% on free plan, $1 + 5% on Basic, none on Pro+
**Best for:** Creators who want solid course hosting without the all-in-one overhead

Teachable has been around since the early days of online education, and it's earned its reputation. The student experience is consistently good — clean course player, progress tracking, certificates, quizzes, and a mobile app that actually works.

**What Teachable does well:**

The course creation workflow is intuitive. You drag-and-drop content into modules and lessons, add videos (hosted on Teachable), quizzes, and text sections. The checkout pages are optimized for conversion. Payment plans, subscriptions, and one-time purchases all work well.

Teachable's affiliate center is functional and easy for your affiliates to use. Payouts are handled through the platform, which removes a lot of administrative friction.

The Basic plan at $39/month is genuinely accessible for new creators — you can build and sell your first course without a huge upfront commitment.

**Where Teachable falls short:**

Marketing tools. Teachable is a course platform, not a marketing platform. You'll still need a separate email marketing tool, landing page builder, and probably a funnel tool if you want to build out a real marketing system. That means more monthly software costs and more things to integrate.

The transaction fees on lower-tier plans are also worth factoring in. If you're selling a $200 course and doing $3,000/month in revenue, 5% + $1 per transaction adds up.

**Who Teachable is right for:**
Creators who want a dedicated, polished course platform and are fine using separate tools for email marketing and funnels.

## Systeme.io — The Budget All-in-One

**Pricing:** Free (up to 1 course, 2,000 contacts), $27/month (Startup), $47/month (Webinar), $97/month (Unlimited)
**Transaction fees:** None
**Best for:** Bootstrapped creators who want everything in one place at a fraction of Kajabi's cost

[Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) is the platform that surprises people most. It's not as polished as Kajabi or as purpose-built for courses as Teachable, but it includes a genuinely impressive amount of functionality at a price that's hard to argue with.

**What Systeme.io does well:**

The platform includes course hosting, email marketing, funnel building, membership sites, affiliate management, and a blog — all in one place, on a free plan that covers real use cases (1 course, 2,000 contacts, 3 funnels).

The course builder lets you create structured courses with video, text, and quizzes. It's not Kajabi-level in terms of polish, but it works. Students get a functional learning experience, and you can drip content on a schedule.

The funnel builder is where Systeme.io really shines relative to its price. You can build sales pages, order bumps, upsells, and downsells without needing ClickFunnels or a separate landing page tool. Email automations are included and work well for basic sequences.

The free plan is genuinely useful — not a crippled demo, but a real platform you can use to launch your first course and make your first sales.

**Where Systeme.io falls short:**

The interface feels less polished than Kajabi or Teachable. The course player is functional but not beautiful. Video is embedded from third-party hosts (YouTube or Vimeo), so you'll need accounts there too. The student experience isn't as seamless as what you get on Kajabi.

Customer support is slower and less robust than the premium platforms. And if you have a specific integration need, Systeme.io's ecosystem is smaller.

**Who Systeme.io is right for:**
Bootstrapped creators who want to launch their first course without spending $100+/month on software, or established creators who want to reduce their tech stack cost.

If you're price-sensitive, [Systeme.io's free plan](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) is the best starting point in the industry right now.

## My Recommendation by Situation

**Just starting out, budget is tight:**
Start with Systeme.io's free plan. Launch your first course, validate demand, and make some money before spending on software. When you outgrow the free tier, upgrade to the $27/month plan.

**Course business is working, want to level up:**
Teachable Pro at $119/month gives you zero transaction fees, a polished student experience, and solid affiliate management. Add a dedicated email tool if you need advanced automation.

**Established creator, want maximum leverage:**
Kajabi at $199/month (Growth) consolidates everything and removes integration headaches. The price is high, but if you're doing $5K+/month, it's justified by the time you save.

**Mid-tier budget, want the Kajabi experience at lower cost:**
This is where Systeme.io's paid plans make sense. For $97/month (Unlimited), you get unlimited courses, unlimited contacts, and every feature on the platform — at half the price of Kajabi's entry tier.
