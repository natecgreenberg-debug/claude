# Best Free Funnel Builders in 2026 (Ranked by What Actually Works)

Building a sales funnel used to mean either spending $300/month on ClickFunnels or stitching together half a dozen tools and praying they didn't break. That's changed. Several platforms now offer legitimate free plans that can take you from zero to actual sales without touching your credit card.

I've tested all the major ones. Here's what's actually worth your time in 2026.

## What Makes a Free Funnel Builder Worth Using?

Before the list: a free plan only matters if it's genuinely usable. I'm looking for:

- Can you build a complete funnel (landing page + thank you page + email capture)?
- Can you collect payments on the free tier?
- What's the contact/traffic limit?
- Are there "free plan" watermarks or branding you can't remove?
- What happens when you hit the limit — is upgrading reasonably priced?

With those filters in mind, here are the platforms that actually pass.

[Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) is the clear winner for anyone who actually wants to run a business on a free plan rather than just test a tool. The free tier isn't a gimped preview — it's a real operating environment.

**The verdict:** If you're serious about building a funnel and not just playing around, start with [Systeme.io's free plan](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67). It's the most complete free offer in the category.

## #2 GrooveFunnels (Groove.cm) — Generous But Unreliable

**Free plan limits:** Unlimited pages, 1 custom domain, 500 contacts

GrooveFunnels made waves a few years ago with an extremely generous free plan and a one-time lifetime deal. The free tier still exists and technically gives you unlimited pages and basic funnel building.

The problems: the platform has been buggy and the company's long-term trajectory feels uncertain. Support has slipped. If you're building something you plan to rely on, the stability concerns are real.

Good for experimenting. Risky for anything you're staking a real campaign on.

**Pros:**
- Unlimited funnel pages
- 1 custom domain on free plan
- One-time deal available (good value if the company survives)

**Cons:**
- Platform stability issues
- Slower editor than competitors
- Uncertain company trajectory

## #3 HubSpot CRM + Landing Pages — Best for B2B Lead Gen

**Free plan limits:** Unlimited contacts, basic landing pages, basic email

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely excellent for B2B lead generation. The catch: it's not really a "funnel builder" in the traditional DTC/info-product sense. There are no order forms, no payment collection, and no course hosting.

What it does brilliantly: capture leads, segment contacts, run email sequences, and integrate with their free CRM. If you're running a service business and your funnel ends with a calendar booking rather than a checkout, HubSpot's free tier is hard to beat.

**Pros:**
- Unlimited contacts on free plan
- Best-in-class CRM integration
- Good email marketing features

**Cons:**
- No payment collection
- HubSpot branding on free tier
- Not designed for product funnels

## #4 Carrd — Best for Simple Lead Gen Pages

**Free plan limits:** 3 sites, carrd.co subdomain, no custom domain, no forms on free tier

Carrd is the simplest tool on this list. You're building single-page sites — landing pages, link-in-bio pages, basic lead capture. The free tier is limited: no custom domain, no form submissions, and the carrd.co subdomain.

The Pro plan ($19/year — yes, per year, not per month) unlocks custom domains and form submissions. At that price it's almost free anyway.

Carrd is not a funnel builder in any complete sense. It's a single-page tool. But if all you need is a beautiful opt-in page or a link tree, it's fast and easy.

**Pros:**
- Extremely fast and simple
- Beautiful templates
- $19/year Pro plan

**Cons:**
- Not a real funnel tool (no upsells, no sequences, no courses)
- Forms locked behind paid plan
- No custom domain on free tier

## #5 Leadpages — Free Trial Only (Not Truly Free)

**Free plan limits:** 14-day free trial, then $37/month minimum

Leadpages is a solid landing page and funnel tool, but it doesn't have a free plan — just a 14-day trial. I'm including it because it shows up in a lot of "free funnel builder" searches and I want to be clear: it's not free.

If you're comparing it as a paid option, Leadpages is competitive with ClickFunnels basic on landing pages but lacks the broader funnel features (no built-in email, no course hosting). At $37/month it's reasonably priced but not the best value given the limitations.
