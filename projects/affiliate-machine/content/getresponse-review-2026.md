# GetResponse Review 2026: Is It Still Worth It?

GetResponse has been around since 1997. That's not a typo — they're one of the oldest email marketing platforms in the game, and they've adapted more than most would expect for a company that age. But being old and being good aren't the same thing. Here's an honest look at where GetResponse stands in 2026.

## Who GetResponse Is Built For

GetResponse positions itself as an all-in-one marketing platform now, not just an email tool. They added landing pages, webinars, conversion funnels, and paid ads features. On paper, it sounds like ClickFunnels plus Mailchimp at a lower price.

The reality: GetResponse is best suited for mid-sized businesses that have outgrown basic email tools and want more automation and segmentation — without the complexity of enterprise platforms like HubSpot or Salesforce Marketing Cloud.

It's especially strong for:
- E-commerce brands using WooCommerce or Shopify
- Webinar-based sales funnels
- Email list monetization at 5,000–50,000 subscribers
- B2B lead nurturing sequences

It's less ideal for solopreneurs on tight budgets (pricing adds up fast) and advanced funnel builders who need deep customization.

## Pricing Breakdown

GetResponse pricing is contact-based and tiers can feel punishing as you grow:

- **Email Marketing plan:** $15.58/month (1,000 contacts), unlimited emails, basic automation
- **Marketing Automation plan:** $48.38/month (1,000 contacts), full automation, webinars (100 attendees)
- **Ecommerce Marketing plan:** $97.58/month (1,000 contacts), abandoned cart, promo codes, transactional emails
- **GetResponse MAX:** Custom pricing (enterprise)

The catch: those prices are for 1,000 contacts. At 5,000 contacts, the Marketing Automation plan jumps to around $78/month. At 10,000 contacts, you're over $115/month. Pricing scales steeply.

Compare that to [Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67), which gives you unlimited emails on all plans and includes funnels, courses, and affiliate management for $27-97/month flat — not contact-based scaling.

## What GetResponse Does Well

### Email Deliverability
This is GetResponse's strongest suit. With nearly 30 years in the email business, their sending infrastructure and ISP relationships are excellent. Deliverability rates consistently rank at the top of third-party tests. If landing in the inbox is your primary concern, GetResponse is a safe bet.

### Automation Builder
The visual automation workflow builder is genuinely good. You can build complex conditional sequences — segment contacts based on behavior, set up time-delayed follow-ups, and trigger automations based on link clicks or purchases. The interface is cleaner than Mailchimp and more accessible than ActiveCampaign for less technical users.

### Webinar Integration
This is a genuinely differentiating feature. GetResponse has a built-in webinar tool (not a Zoom integration — actual native webinars) included in higher plans. For businesses that use webinar-based selling, this removes a $100-200/month tool from your stack. Easywebinar and Demio both charge that for equivalent functionality.

### Landing Pages and Funnels
The conversion funnel feature (they call it "Autofunnel") lets you build lead magnet → sales page → checkout → upsell sequences. It's functional and the templates are decent, though less extensive than ClickFunnels. For light funnel needs, it handles the job.

### Ecommerce Features
The ecommerce plan has abandoned cart recovery, product recommendations, promo codes, and transactional emails. If you're running WooCommerce or Shopify, the native integrations are solid. This isn't Klaviyo territory, but for businesses not needing advanced predictive analytics, it's sufficient.

## What GetResponse Does Poorly

### Pricing Transparency and Value
The contact-based pricing makes it difficult to budget. If your list grows unexpectedly — say from a viral opt-in campaign — your bill can jump significantly mid-cycle. The base plan also locks features you'd expect to be standard (like abandoned cart and advanced segmentation) behind the more expensive tiers.

### Landing Page Editor
The landing page builder lags behind dedicated tools. The templates feel dated compared to Leadpages or even Systeme.io, and the editor can be sluggish. This has improved but hasn't fully caught up.

### Support Quality
GetResponse offers 24/7 live chat on most plans, which sounds great. In practice, the support quality is inconsistent — first-line agents often give generic responses and escalations to technical teams can take 24-48 hours. For a platform you're relying on for revenue, that lag is frustrating.

### Template Design
Email templates and landing page designs lean corporate and generic. If you're in a creative industry or building a premium brand, you'll likely end up doing more custom design work than you'd want.

## GetResponse vs. The Alternatives

**GetResponse vs. Mailchimp:** GetResponse wins on automation depth and the webinar tool. Mailchimp has a better free plan (500 contacts) and stronger e-commerce analytics at scale. For list sizes under 5,000 with moderate automation needs, it's close.

**GetResponse vs. ActiveCampaign:** ActiveCampaign beats GetResponse on CRM integration and automation complexity. GetResponse wins on webinars and slightly on price at lower contact volumes.

**GetResponse vs. Systeme.io:** [Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) is significantly cheaper, includes funnel building, course hosting, and affiliate management in a single platform. GetResponse has better email deliverability infrastructure and native webinars. If you're primarily an email marketer with a large list, GetResponse makes sense. If you're building a business with funnels, courses, and products, Systeme.io gives you more for less money.

**GetResponse vs. Klaviyo:** Klaviyo dominates for serious e-commerce stores — better segmentation, better predictive analytics, better SMS. GetResponse's ecommerce features are a budget alternative, not a true Klaviyo competitor.

## The Verdict

GetResponse is a solid, mature platform with genuinely strong deliverability and a useful webinar tool. It's not cheap, and the contact-based pricing can catch you off guard as you grow. For businesses in the 5,000–25,000 subscriber range that run regular webinars and need robust automation, it's a reasonable choice.

For solopreneurs, course creators, and early-stage online businesses, the pricing becomes harder to justify — especially when platforms like [Systeme.io](https://systeme.io/?sa=sa024254513373376fa2a167f3d85d4c75c0537d67) bundle email marketing with funnels, courses, and affiliate tools at a fraction of the cost.

**Rating: 3.8 / 5**

Best for webinar-heavy businesses and established email marketers who prioritize deliverability. Not the best value for beginners or all-in-one needs.
