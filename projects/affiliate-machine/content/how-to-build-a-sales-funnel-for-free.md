## Step 2: Create Your Funnel

In the left sidebar, click **Funnels** → **Create**.

Name your funnel (something descriptive like "Lead Magnet - [Your Topic]").

Choose a funnel template type. For a standard lead generation funnel, you want:

- **Thank You Page** — what people see after opting in

Click "Create" and you'll see your funnel map with two steps.

## Step 3: Build Your Landing Page (Squeeze Page)

Click on the Squeeze Page step → Edit Page.

This opens the drag-and-drop editor. The interface is straightforward:

**Choose a template.** Systeme.io has pre-built templates for most funnel types. Pick one that's close to what you want — you'll customize everything anyway. Look for a template with a strong headline area, a bulleted benefit list, and an opt-in form.

**Customize the headline.** This is the most important element on your page. A headline that works follows this structure:
> "How to [Desired Outcome] Without [Common Frustration] in [Timeframe]"

Examples:
- "How to Get Your First 1,000 Email Subscribers Without Paid Ads in 30 Days"
- "How to Write a Sales Email in 20 Minutes That Actually Converts"
- "How to Lose 10 Pounds Without Giving Up Your Favorite Foods"

**Add your benefit bullets.** Three to five specific outcomes they'll get from your lead magnet. Be concrete — "you'll learn how to do X" is weaker than "the exact template I used to do X."

**Customize the opt-in form.** In Systeme.io, the form is already connected to your contact list. By default it collects first name and email. You can remove the name field to reduce friction (generally increases opt-in rates).

**Add your lead magnet image.** A mockup of your PDF/checklist/video adds visual credibility. Canva has free book mockup templates if you need one.

**Set the button text.** "Get Instant Access" or "Send Me the [Lead Magnet Name]" consistently outperforms "Submit" or "Sign Up."

Click Save.

## Step 4: Build Your Thank You Page

Back in the funnel view, click on the Thank You Page step → Edit Page.

This is what people see immediately after opting in. Keep it simple:

- Confirm they're signed up: "You're in! Check your email for [Lead Magnet Name]."
- Tell them what to do next: "While you wait, here's a quick message from me..." or a short video.
- Optional: Add a tripwire offer — a low-ticket product ($7-27) relevant to what they just opted in for. Many people buy immediately after opting in because purchase intent is high.

If you're adding a tripwire, add an order button (in Systeme.io: Add Element → Order Button). Connect it to a product you'll create in the next step.

---

## Step 5: Set Up Your Email Sequence

Go to **Emails** → **Campaigns** → **Create Campaign**.

Name it something like "Welcome Sequence - [Lead Magnet]."

Add your first email. Set the delay to 0 days (sends immediately after signup). This email should:
- Deliver the lead magnet (upload it to Systeme.io and link it, or link to a Google Drive/Dropbox file)
- Introduce yourself briefly
- Tell them what to expect from future emails

Add a second email (delay: 1 day). This should provide additional value related to the lead magnet topic. Build credibility.

Add a third email (delay: 3 days). This can begin a soft sell — introduce a related paid product or service, tell a story about a result you or a client got, and include a call to action.

**Connecting the sequence to your funnel:**
Back in your funnel, click on the Squeeze Page step → select **Automation Rules**. Add a rule: "When contact opts in → Subscribe to campaign: [Your Welcome Sequence]."

Now every opt-in will automatically enter your email sequence.

## Step 6: Set Up Payment (If Selling a Product)

If you want to sell a paid product through your funnel, go to **Products** → **Create Product**.

Fill in:
- Product name and description
- Price (one-time or subscription)
- Payment processor: connect Stripe or PayPal (both are free to set up)

Create an order page: back in your funnel, add a new step between your Squeeze Page and Thank You Page (or use it as a standalone sales funnel). Choose the "Order Page" template.

In the order page editor, connect the product you just created.

Systeme.io handles the checkout, payment processing, and delivery confirmation automatically.

## Step 7: Test Everything

Before you launch, run through the funnel yourself:

1. Visit your landing page URL (find it in the funnel settings)
2. Enter a test email address (use a second email you have access to)
3. Confirm you land on the thank you page
4. Check your test inbox — did the welcome email arrive? Does the lead magnet link work?
5. If selling: run a test purchase ($0 can be used to test the checkout flow)

Fix anything that breaks. This step saves embarrassment.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Headline vagueness.** "Free guide to marketing" is not a headline. Be specific about the outcome.

**Too many form fields.** More fields = fewer opt-ins. Start with email only. Add name if you want to personalize.

**Skipping the email sequence.** The landing page gets them in. The email sequence builds trust and generates revenue. Don't skip it.

**Launching without testing.** I cannot count the number of times I've seen someone launch a funnel where the confirmation email never sent or the lead magnet link was broken. Test it yourself first.

**Driving cold traffic to a product offer immediately.** If someone doesn't know you, lead with the free thing. The sequence warms them up for the sale.
