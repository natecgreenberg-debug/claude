# Short-Form Script Research — 2026-03-16

## Purpose
Research to inform 60 scripts for Kate Mercer AI influencer account (menopause niche, TikTok/Reels/Shorts).

---

## Key Findings

### The First 3 Seconds Are Everything
- 65% of viewers who make it past the first 3 seconds will watch to 10 seconds
- 45% of those will stick around for 30 seconds
- The brain "thin-slices" — draws conclusions from brief exposures
- You have one sentence to stop the scroll. Everything else is secondary.

### Psychological Triggers That Work
Three primary triggers drive hook performance:
1. **Pattern Interruption** — breaks expected flow; brain flags it as novel
2. **Curiosity Gap** — withholds information the viewer's brain wants to complete
3. **Social Proof** — implies others have found this valuable

Best hooks combine at least two triggers. Urgency + relatability activates the brain's reward system, making viewers 2.5x more likely to keep watching.

### Hook Type Taxonomy

| Hook Type | Formula | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Stat Hook | "X% of women don't know that..." | Surprising, counterintuitive facts |
| Question Hook | "Did your doctor ever tell you about...?" | Creates immediate information void |
| Myth-Bust Hook | "Everything you've been told about X is wrong" | High credibility play, stops skeptics |
| Story Hook | "I was sitting in my doctor's office when..." | Emotional pull, relatability |
| "Nobody Talks About" | "Nobody tells you that after 50, your..." | Validation + curiosity |
| Pattern Interrupt | Start mid-sentence, or with a provocative claim | Scroll-stopping |
| Before/After | "This is what happened after I..." | Result-first framing |
| "I Didn't Know" | "I had no idea that X was causing Y" | Peer solidarity, not authority |
| Direct Address | "If you're over 45 and struggling with..." | Instant audience self-selection |

### Script Structure for 20-30 Second Clips (60-80 words)

**Proven 4-part formula:**
1. **Hook** (0-3 sec / 10-15 words): Stops the scroll. Bold claim, question, or pattern interrupt.
2. **Agitate or Contextualize** (3-12 sec / 20-30 words): Validate the pain or deepen the curiosity. "And here's the thing nobody tells you..."
3. **Value/Insight** (12-22 sec / 20-30 words): The one specific fact, tip, or reframe. Concrete, not vague.
4. **CTA** (22-30 sec / 5-10 words): Soft close. "Follow for more." "Save this." "Link in bio."

**Word count target**: 60-80 words for natural 20-30 sec delivery at ~150 wpm speaking pace.

### What Makes Health/Menopause Content Perform
- **Validation first** — women 45-60 have been dismissed by doctors and culture; content that says "your experience is real" hits hard
- **Peer, not authority** — "I figured this out" outperforms "the research shows" for this audience
- **Specific over general** — "progesterone dropped 80% in perimenopause" beats "hormone levels change"
- **Name the thing they're experiencing** — "word-finding" or "brain fog" or "midnight wake-up" = instant recognition
- **Permission hooks** — giving them permission to feel what they feel ("you're not crazy") = high shares
- **FDA black box removal (Nov 2025)** — major real-world hook for HRT content; changes the whole narrative

### What Women 45-60 Respond To
- "I thought it was just me" moments
- Practical tips they can act on today
- Validation that their body is not broken
- Being spoken to as intelligent adults, not patients
- Information their doctor didn't give them
- Peer lived experience over clinical authority

### Retention Tactics
- One idea per video — never pack two insights in
- 15-22 seconds is the retention sweet spot; longer is okay for high-value education
- Text overlays matter — 70% watch without sound initially
- End on a cliffhanger or "save this" to drive replay/saves
- Series content: "Part 2" drives strong follow-through from Part 1 viewers
- 60%+ completion rate = strong signal to algorithm; 70%+ = significantly higher reach

### Script Format Rules Applied
- NO "Hi I'm Kate" intros — jump straight into the hook
- Kate speaks TO camera like a peer, not a lecturer
- One specific fact or insight per video — no kitchen-sink content
- CTA is always soft: follow, save, link in bio, "I cover this in my guide"
- Varied hook types across the 60 scripts for feed diversity
